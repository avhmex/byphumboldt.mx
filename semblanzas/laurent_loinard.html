Laurent Loinard

Semblanza, 2025

Laurent Loinard is an astrophysicist at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), specializing in star formation and early stellar evolution, the interstellar medium and its chemistry, and the environments of supermassive black holes. He primarily uses radio interferometry and is internationally recognized as a pioneer in applying this technique to measure ultra-precise distances to celestial objects. He is a member of the Event Horizon Telescope (EHT) consortium, which produced the first images of the black holes at the centers of the Milky Way and the nearby galaxy Messier 87. Over the course of his career, he has published nearly 250 peer-reviewed research articles, which have received over 22,000 citations in the international literature. His Hirsch index (h-index) currently stands at 65.

In addition to the 2011 Bessel Prize from the Alexander von Humboldt Foundation, Laurent Loinard has received several prestigious distinctions, including the National University Prize for Young Academics (Premio Universidad Nacional para Jóvenes Académicos) in 2007, a Guggenheim Fellowship from the John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation (USA) in 2010, a Marcos Moshinsky Fellowship at UNAM in 2013, the 2022 Prize of The World Academy of Sciences (TWAS) in the category of Astrophysics, Earth and Space Sciences, and the 2024 Research Prize of the Mexican Physics Society. As a member of the EHT consortium, he is also a co-recipient of numerous international honors, including the 2020 Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics, the 2020 Einstein Medal, and the 2021 Group Award from the Royal Astronomical Society of the United Kingdom.

As a result of the Bessel Prize, Laurent Loinard spent two sabbatical visits at the Max Planck Institute for Radio Astronomy (MPIfR) in Bonn, corresponding to the academic years 2011–2012 and 2015–2016. He has maintained a close collaboration with the MPIfR, where several of his former students have completed postdoctoral or extended research visits, including one as a Humboldt Fellow.

Laurent Loinard is currently the 2024–2025 Robert F. Kennedy Visiting Professor of Astrophysics at Harvard University, with dual appointments at the David Rockefeller Center for Latin American Studies and the Black Hole Initiative.
