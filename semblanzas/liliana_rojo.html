Liliana Carolina Rojo

Semblanza, 2025

Centro de Investigaciones Biológicas del Noroeste, SC

Marine biologist specializing in marine biotechnology. She has conducted research across diverse fields, from dolphin genetics and neglected diseases to the exploration of crustacean enzymes, from their genes to their function. Her work focuses on the sustainable transformation of marine waste into high-value products, leveraging the unique potential of marine enzymes, often found in fisheries and aquaculture by-products. During 2023-2024 she was awarded the Georg Forster Research Fellowship, which enabled her to carry out collaborative research with the Alfred Wegener Institute, her research project focused on combining two different sources of marine waste (algae biomass and marine enzymes) in a complementary process to obtain value-added products. She is currently leading projects that aim to turn scientific knowledge into innovative solutions for today's challenges and for generations to come. Her driving force is to catalyze the power of marine science to meet human needs while respecting the planet's boundaries.
